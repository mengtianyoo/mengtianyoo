Research

Research Interests and Approach

This page can function like a simple research statement: what topics you care about, how you approach them, and what kinds of questions you want to study next.

Research Themes

Machine Learning

I am interested in data-driven modeling, evaluation design, and how to make model behavior easier to understand and reproduce.

Natural Language Processing

I care about multilingual text understanding, research assistants for reading and summarization, and practical NLP workflows.

Data Mining

I am also interested in how patterns can be discovered from structured and unstructured data in meaningful application settings.

Methods and Workflow

I prefer research workflows that are incremental, readable, and easy to revisit. That means carefully recording literature notes, designing experiments with explicit questions in mind, and preserving code and results in a way that supports later revision or collaboration.

In practice, I am particularly interested in the space between exploratory work and formal results: how ideas move from notes to prototypes, then to experiments, and eventually to papers or systems.

Application Domains

  • Research support tools for literature review and note organization
  • Multilingual text analytics and document understanding
  • Data storytelling, reporting, and explainable analysis workflows
  • Academic and interdisciplinary projects where reproducibility matters
